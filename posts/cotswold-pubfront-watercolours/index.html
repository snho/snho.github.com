UK Pub Fronts

Background

Over the initial lockdowns in 2020, I started to get into watercolour paintings. I’ve been a fan of the look of watercolour paintings for as long as I can remember, and I’ve always enjoyed the difficulty of them and how the process is quite different to other painting mediums. The difference being how it’s an additive process in that you go from light to dark traditional and due to the less opaque nature of the paints, it’s easier to mess up and hard to undo. Unlike acrylics and oils, you can pretty much go over your mistakes.

This different approach, mixed with the difficulty, and also the fact that I can’t really undo my mistakes as easily as the other mediums (or with digital art), was a real draw to me. It’s the opposite in how I normally operate, thus it was the perfect medium to get me to slow down and not rush things.

On my last Japan trip I picked up a basic student grade watercolour set from Sakura. I think I got it from Tokyu Hands. I could literally spend a whole day in Japan’s multistory stationary and arts & craft stores… and I have…

Inspiration

The first series of paintings I complete was a collection of pub fronts. The series is mostly inspired with the Tokyo Storefronts series by . Obviously, I had to put my own spin on it, and thus I chose to reflect on and paint the pub fronts of the pubs and inns of mostly around the small British Cotswald town of Burford, where I was staying at for a wedding in 2019.
